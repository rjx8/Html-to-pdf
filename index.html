Nikola Tesla: Timeline of Genius

The Life and Revolutionary Inventions of the Master of Electricity

Nikola Tesla (1856-1943) was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current (AC) electricity supply system. His revolutionary inventions and visionary ideas laid the foundation for many technologies we use today.
1856
Birth in Smiljan
Born during a lightning storm in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia). His mother predicted he would be "a child of light."
1875
Austrian Polytechnic
Enrolled at Austrian Polytechnic in Graz, studying mathematics, physics, and mechanics with exceptional grades.
1882
AC Motor Concept
Conceived the idea of the rotating magnetic field and AC induction motor while walking in Budapest's city park.
1884
Arrival in America
Immigrated to the United States with little money but carrying a letter of recommendation to Thomas Edison.
1888
AC Motor Patent
Received patents for his AC induction motor and polyphase system, revolutionizing electrical power transmission.
1891
Tesla Coil Invention
Invented the Tesla coil, a high-frequency transformer that became fundamental to wireless technology and radio.
1893
World's Fair Triumph
Demonstrated AC power at Chicago World's Fair, proving its superiority over DC in the "War of Currents."
1895
Niagara Falls Power
His AC system powered the first major hydroelectric plant at Niagara Falls, lighting up Buffalo, New York.
1899-1900
Colorado Springs Laboratory
Conducted wireless power transmission experiments with his "Magnifying Transmitter," creating artificial lightning.
1901
Wardenclyffe Tower
Began construction of Wardenclyffe Tower for worldwide wireless communication and power transmission.
1943
Legacy Lives On
Died in New York City, leaving behind over 300 patents and ideas that continue to inspire modern technology.
